The Transmission

The Landauer-Büttiker transmission, T(E)T(E)T(E), describes how a particle with energy EEE from a source band, sss, transmits through the graph to a drain band, ddd. When the bands are filled with particles according to some distribution functions fs/d(E)f_{s/d} (E)f​s/d​​(E) the total particle current from source to drain is given by:

Isd=∫−∞∞dEfs(E)(1−fd(E))Tsd(E) I_{sd} = \int_{-\infty}^\infty \mathrm{d}E \, f_s(E) (1 - f_d (E)) T_{sd}(E) I​sd​​=∫​−∞​∞​​dEf​s​​(E)(1−f​d​​(E))T​sd​​(E)

The transmission widget

In our graph the strength of the dashed line link between nodes and directly encodes t=πgt = \sqrt{\pi} gt=√​π​​​g.

The coupling strengths πg\sqrt{\pi} g√​π​​​g is encoded directly in the link strengths. So when you are tuning the strength of the dashed line, you are essentially tuning πg\sqrt{\pi} g√​π​​​g. The reason for the π\sqrt{\pi}√​π​​​ pre-factor follows from a desire to have t=1t = 1t=1 matching the intermediate coupling strength.

The transmission widgets allow to plot the transmission between two arbitrary bands for each energy EEE. To add a new transmission press the + icon, and choosing the relevant bands. More transmission functions can be added to the plot, and you can toggle an edit dialog simply by clicking the legend for each line.

One can also modify the transmission plots, by cutting the functions up between two eigenlevels, m and m+1m \text{ and } m+1m and m+1, and pulling them apart by an amount, δ\deltaδ. Mathematically we are replacing the graph Hamiltonian by the transformed operator,

H~graph=V†D~V \tilde{H}_{\text{graph}} = V^\dagger \tilde{D} V ​H​~​​​graph​​=V​†​​​D​~​​V Here VVV contains the usual graph eigenvectors, and D~=∑i≤m(Ei−δ/2)b^i†b^i+∑i>m(Ei+δ/2)b^i†b^i. \tilde{D} = \sum_{i \leq m} (E_i - \delta/2) \hat{b}^\dagger_i \hat{b}_i + \sum_{i > m} (E_i + \delta/2) \hat{b}^\dagger_i \hat{b}_i. ​D​~​​=​i≤m​∑​​(E​i​​−δ/2)​b​^​​​i​†​​​b​^​​​i​​+​i>m​∑​​(E​i​​+δ/2)​b​^​​​i​†​​​b​^​​​i​​.

Beware: Do not trust results obtained by cutting in between two degenerate (with the same eigenenergy) levels, since the diagonalization algorithm does not explicitly symmetrize degenerate eigenvectors.
